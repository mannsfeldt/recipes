Copycat Panera Broccoli Cheddar Soup

A homemade take on the Panera classic — broccoli, carrots, and celery in a thick, sharp-Cheddar broth. Great in a bread bowl.

  • Prep10 min
  • Cook35 min
  • Total45 min
  • Yield~8 cups
  • Servings8

Equipment

  • Skillet
  • Large saucepan
  • Whisk
Scale recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp (14 g) butter, for the onion
  • 1/2 (75 g) onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup (57 g) butter, melted
  • 1/4 cup (30 g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups (480 g) milk
  • 2 cups (480 g) chicken stock
  • 1 1/2 cups (135 g) broccoli florets, coarsely chopped
  • 1 cup (110 g) matchstick-cut carrots
  • 1 stalk (40 g) celery, thinly sliced
  • 2 1/2 cups (283 g) sharp Cheddar, shredded
  • 1/4 tsp (1 g) salt, to taste
  • 1/8 tsp (1 g) black pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. 1

    Melt the 1 tbsp butter in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the onion and cook, stirring, until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Set aside.

  2. 2

    In a large saucepan over medium-low heat, whisk together the melted butter and flour. Keep whisking and cooking 3–4 minutes, adding a tablespoon or two of the milk if needed to keep the flour from browning.

  3. 3

    Gradually whisk in the remaining milk, then stir in the chicken stock and bring to a simmer.

  4. 4

    Cook, stirring, until thickened, about 15–20 minutes.

  5. 5

    Add the broccoli, carrots, celery, and reserved onion. Simmer until the vegetables are tender, about 10–15 minutes.

  6. 6

    Stir in the Cheddar until melted, then season with salt and pepper to taste and serve.

Notes: Add the cheese off the heat, a handful at a time, to keep it from clumping. Many cooks prefer to build the roux with the stock first, simmer the vegetables in the broth to develop flavor, and stir in the milk and cheese at the end so the dairy doesn’t scorch. A pinch of nutmeg and a clove of garlic are popular additions.
