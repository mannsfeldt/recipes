Banana Nut Bread

A wholesome loaf using both all-purpose and whole wheat flour, loaded with ripe banana sweetness and a satisfying crunch of walnuts and pecans.

  • Prep15 min
  • Bake1 hr 15 min
  • Total1 hr 30 min
  • Yield1 loaf

Equipment

  • 9×5-inch loaf pan
  • Two mixing bowls
Scale recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 medium (200 g)ripe bananas, mashed
  • 3/4 cup (165 g)brown sugar
  • 1 (50 g)egg
  • 1 cup (240 ml)milk
  • 1/3 cup (75 g)butter, melted
  • 1 cup (125 g)all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup (120 g)whole wheat flour
  • 1 tbsp (12 g)baking powder
  • 1 tsp (2.5 g)ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp (1.5 g)salt
  • 3/4 cup (90 g)chopped walnuts and/or pecans

Instructions

  1. 1

    Preheat oven to 350 °F. Grease and flour a 9×5-inch loaf pan.

  2. 2

    In a large bowl, whisk together mashed bananas, brown sugar, egg, milk, and melted butter until smooth.

  3. 3

    In a separate bowl, stir together all-purpose flour, whole wheat flour, baking powder, cinnamon, salt, and nuts.

  4. 4

    Pour the wet ingredients into the dry and fold together until just combined — a few streaks of flour are fine. Do not overmix or the loaf will be tough.

  5. 5

    Pour batter into the prepared pan. Bake 1 to 1¼ hours until a toothpick inserted in the centre comes out clean. If the top browns too quickly, tent with foil after 45 minutes.

  6. 6

    Cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then turn out onto a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

Notes: The riper the bananas (black spots are ideal), the sweeter and more flavourful the bread. This loaf freezes well — slice first for easy grab-and-go.
