Butter Tarts

A quintessential Canadian bake — flaky pastry shells filled with a gooey brown sugar and corn syrup mixture studded with plump raisins. Irresistible warm from the oven.

  • Prep20 min
  • Bake15 min
  • Total40 min
  • Yield12–16 tarts

Equipment

  • Muffin tin or tart tins
  • Pastry brush
Scale recipe

Ingredients

Filling

  • 1 cup (200 g)brown sugar
  • 1 cup (320 ml)corn syrup
  • 1/2 cup (115 g)butter or margarine, melted
  • 2 (100 g)eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 tsp (4 g)vanilla extract
  • 1 pinch (1 g)salt
  • 1 cup (150 g)raisins

Pastry

  • 12 (1 g)unbaked tart shells (homemade or store-bought frozen)

Instructions

  1. 1

    Place raisins in a heatproof bowl and pour boiling water over them. Let stand 5 minutes to plump and warm the bowl, then drain thoroughly.

  2. 2

    To the warm drained bowl, add melted butter, brown sugar, and corn syrup. Stir to combine.

  3. 3

    Add beaten eggs, vanilla, and salt. Stir well until the filling is smooth.

  4. 4

    Line tart tins with pastry shells. Fill each shell about two-thirds full with the raisin filling — do not overfill or the filling will boil over.

  5. 5

    Bake in a 450 °F oven for 5–8 minutes until the pastry starts to set and turn golden.

  6. 6

    Reduce oven temperature to 350 °F and continue baking until the filling is just set — it should wobble slightly in the centre but not be liquid. Watch carefully and remove before the filling boils over the edges.

  7. 7

    Cool in the tins for 10 minutes before removing. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Notes: The two-stage bake (high then low) sets the pastry quickly before the filling has a chance to bubble over. Slightly runny filling is prized — do not overbake. A runnier filling means less egg; a firmer one means more.
