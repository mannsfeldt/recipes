Almond Sugar Cookies

A classic European-style cut-out cookie, lightly spiced with cinnamon and cloves, with a subtle almond flavour and a tender crumb.

  • Prep20 min
  • Chill30 min
  • Bake12 min
  • Yield~36 cookies

Equipment

  • Stand or hand mixer
  • Rolling pin
  • Cookie cutters
  • Baking sheet
Scale recipe

Ingredients

  • 500 g (500 g)all-purpose flour
  • 2 tsp (8 g)baking powder
  • 250 g (250 g)sugar
  • 1 tsp (4 g)vanilla extract
  • 1/8 tsp (0.3 g)ground cloves
  • 1 tsp (2.5 g)ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp (1 g)almond extract
  • 3 (150 g)eggs
  • 200 g (200 g)butter, softened
  • 100 g (100 g)ground almonds or hazelnuts

Instructions

  1. 1

    In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, sugar, ground cloves, and cinnamon.

  2. 2

    Make a well in the centre and add butter, eggs, vanilla extract, and almond extract. Work together with your hands or a mixer until a smooth dough forms.

  3. 3

    Mix in the ground almonds. Flatten the dough into a disc, wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  4. 4

    Preheat oven to 350 °F. On a lightly floured surface, roll dough to about ¼-inch thickness. Cut into shapes with cookie cutters.

  5. 5

    Place on a parchment-lined baking sheet and bake 10–12 minutes until the edges are just turning golden. Cool on the pan for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

Notes: These keep well in an airtight tin for up to 2 weeks — the flavour actually improves after a day or two. Dust with icing sugar or drizzle with a simple glaze for a festive touch.
