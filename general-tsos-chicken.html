General Tso's Chicken

Sweet, savory, spicy, and tangy crispy chicken in a glossy Chinese-American sauce — better than takeout and on the table in under an hour.

  • Prep15 min
  • Marinate30 min
  • Cook15 min
  • Total60 min
  • Servings4–5

Equipment

  • Deep skillet or large pot (for frying)
  • Deep-fry or instant-read thermometer
  • Colander
Scale recipe

Ingredients

Sauce

  • 3 tbsp (45 g) soy sauce, light or all-purpose
  • 1 tbsp (18 g) hoisin sauce
  • 1 tbsp (15 g) rice vinegar (sub: white wine vinegar)
  • 2 tsp (10 g) chilli paste (e.g. Sambal Oelek)
  • 1 tsp (4 g) toasted sesame oil
  • 3 tbsp (40 g) brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp (8 g) cornstarch (for sauce)
  • ¾ cup (180 g) low-sodium chicken stock

Chicken

  • 1.4 lb (600 g) boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 tsp (5 g) fresh ginger, finely grated (for marinade)
  • 1 tsp (3 g) garlic, finely grated (for marinade)
  • 1 cup (120 g) cornstarch (for coating)
  • 2 cups (436 g) neutral oil for frying (peanut, vegetable, or canola — adjust for depth; see notes)

Stir Fry

  • 2 tbsp (28 g) neutral oil
  • 2 cloves (10 g) garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 tsp (10 g) fresh ginger, finely chopped
  • · tsp (1 g) red chilli flakes
  • 2 stalks (20 g) green onion, finely sliced, to garnish
  • 1 tsp (3 g) sesame seeds, to garnish

Instructions

Sauce

  1. 1

    In a bowl, whisk together soy sauce, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, chilli paste, and sesame oil. This is your sauce-marinade base.

Chicken

  1. 2

    Measure out 2 tablespoons of the sauce-marinade base into a bowl with the chicken thighs. Add the finely grated ginger and garlic (for marinade), toss to coat, and marinate for 30 minutes. Meanwhile, whisk brown sugar, cornstarch (for sauce), and chicken stock into the remaining sauce-marinade base; set aside.

  2. 3

    Add the cornstarch (for coating) to the marinated chicken and toss until every piece is fully and separately coated. Tip into a colander and shake off the excess — a thick coating can taste powdery and will cloud the frying oil quickly.

  3. 4

    Heat neutral oil for frying in a deep skillet to 200°C / 390°F, with enough oil to come at least halfway up the chicken. Fry in batches for 3 minutes, turning halfway, until golden and crispy. Drain on paper towels.

Stir Fry

  1. 5

    Wipe out the skillet or use a clean one. Heat the neutral oil over medium heat. Add the finely chopped garlic, ginger, and red chilli flakes. Sauté 30 seconds until the garlic is light golden and fragrant.

  2. 6

    Pour in the finished sauce, bring to a simmer, and stir occasionally until it thickens enough that you can draw a clear path across the pan base, about 2 minutes.

  3. 7

    Add the fried chicken and toss quickly to coat — the sooner you plate it, the crispier it stays. Transfer to a serving plate, scatter with green onion and sesame seeds, and serve immediately with steamed rice.

Notes: Use light or all-purpose soy sauce only — dark soy is too intense and darkens*the sauce. Chicken thighs are strongly preferred over breast; if using breast or tenderloins, add ¼ tsp baking soda to the marinade and marinate 1 hour to tenderize. For the stir fry aromatics, finely chop garlic and ginger with a knife — grated paste turns to burn quickly in a hot pan. Shake excess cornstarch well; too thick a coating tastes powdery and clouds the oil. Use enough frying oil to come halfway up the chicken (shallow fry), or float the chicken (deep fry) for even crispiness all around. Leftovers keep 5 days refrigerated; reheat with a splash of water to loosen the sauce, though the chicken won't be crispy.
