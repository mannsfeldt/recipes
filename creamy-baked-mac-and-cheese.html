Creamy Baked Mac and Cheese

A contest-winning baked macaroni and cheese: a rich cheddar-and-Gruyère sauce tossed with pasta, layered with more cheese, and baked until bubbly.

  • Prep20 min
  • Cook15 min
  • Total35 min
  • Yield9×13″ dish
  • Servings8

Equipment

  • 9×13″ (3 qt) baking dish
  • Large pot
  • Large saucepan
  • Box grater
  • Whisk
Scale recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (454 g) dried elbow pasta
  • 4 cups (452 g) medium cheddar, shredded (16 oz block)
  • 2 cups (226 g) Gruyère, shredded (8 oz block)
  • 1/2 cup (113 g) unsalted butter
  • 1/2 cup (60 g) all-purpose flour
  • 2 1/2 cups (600 g) half and half, room temperature
  • 1 1/2 cups (360 g) whole milk, room temperature
  • 1/2 tbsp (9 g) salt
  • 1/2 tsp (1 g) black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp (1 g) paprika (smoked, if you like)

Instructions

  1. 1

    Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C) and grease a 3-qt (9×13″) baking dish.

  2. 2

    Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook 1 minute less than the package directs for al dente. Drain and toss with a little olive oil so it doesn’t stick.

  3. 3

    While the water heats, shred the cheddar and Gruyère and toss them together, then divide into three piles: about 3 cups for the sauce, 1½ cups for the middle layer, and 1½ cups for the top.

  4. 4

    Melt the butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and cook about 1 minute, until it looks like wet sand. Slowly pour in about 2 cups of the half and half, whisking constantly until smooth, then whisk in the remaining half and half and the whole milk until smooth.

  5. 5

    Keep cooking over medium heat, whisking often, until very thick — about the consistency of a slightly thinned condensed soup.

  6. 6

    Off the heat, stir in the salt, pepper, and paprika, then add 3 cups of the cheese in two additions, stirring until melted and smooth.

  7. 7

    In a large bowl, stir the drained pasta into the cheese sauce. Pour half into the baking dish, sprinkle with 1½ cups cheese, then spread the remaining pasta on top.

  8. 8

    Scatter the last 1½ cups cheese over the top and bake 15 minutes, until bubbly and lightly golden.

Notes: Shred the cheese from blocks — pre-shredded bagged cheese is coated with starch that keeps it from melting smoothly. Use room-temperature dairy so the sauce thickens faster. For a browned, crunchier top, bake at 350–375°F for 20–30 minutes and broil 2–5 minutes at the end. Original recipe yields 8–10 servings.
