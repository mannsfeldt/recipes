Turkey (or Chicken) Carcass Soup

The best use of a holiday bird carcass — a long-simmered broth packed with carrots, celery, noodles, and every last bit of good meat.

  • Prep20 min
  • Simmer3 hr+
  • Total3 hr 30 min
  • Servings8–10

Equipment

  • Large stockpot
  • Fine mesh strainer
Scale recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 (1 g)poultry carcass (turkey or chicken)
  • 1 pkg (10 g)chicken bouillon (or 2 cubes)
  • 1 pkg (375 g) (375 g)medium egg noodles
  • 6 medium (500 g)carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 1/2 bunch (200 g)celery, chopped

Instructions

  1. 1

    Place the carcass in a large stockpot and cover completely with cold water. Bring to a boil over high heat, skimming any foam that rises to the surface.

  2. 2

    Reduce heat and simmer for at least 2 hours to extract maximum flavour from the bones. The longer the better.

  3. 3

    Remove the carcass and set aside to cool slightly. Strain the broth through a fine mesh strainer back into the pot, discarding the solids. Pick any good meat off the carcass and return it to the pot.

  4. 4

    Stir in the bouillon and bring the broth back to a boil. Taste and season with salt and pepper.

  5. 5

    Add the noodles and cook for 5 minutes.

  6. 6

    Add the carrots and celery. Return to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for at least 1 hour until vegetables are tender and soup is flavourful.

Notes: Make the broth up to 3 days ahead and refrigerate — the fat will solidify on top and lift off easily, giving you a cleaner soup. This freezes beautifully without the noodles (add fresh noodles when reheating).
