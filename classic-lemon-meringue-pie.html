Classic Lemon Meringue Pie

A par-baked crust, a tart-sweet lemon filling thickened with cornstarch and tempered yolks, and a billowy French meringue toasted golden. Written for a store-bought crust — the partial blind bake is built right in.

  • Prep30 min
  • Cook1 hr
  • Chill5 hr
  • Total~6.5 hr
  • Yield1 (9") pie
  • Servings8

Equipment

  • 9" pie dish (if not baked in a store tin)
  • Parchment paper
  • Pie weights or dried beans
  • Medium saucepan
  • Whisk
  • Handheld or stand mixer + whisk attachment
  • Citrus zester
  • Wire cooling rack
Scale recipe

Ingredients

Crust

  • 1 shell (200 g) premade 9-inch pie crust, thawed if frozen (deep-dish preferred)

Lemon filling

  • 5 large (90 g) egg yolks (save the whites for the meringue)
  • 1 1/3 cups (320 g) water
  • 1 cup (200 g) granulated sugar
  • 1/3 cup (38 g) cornstarch
  • 1/4 tsp (1.5 g) salt
  • 1/2 cup (120 g) fresh lemon juice (about 3 lemons)
  • 1 tbsp (6 g) lemon zest
  • 2 tbsp (28 g) unsalted butter, softened

Meringue

  • 5 large (165 g) egg whites, at room temperature
  • 1/2 tsp (1.5 g) cream of tartar
  • 1/2 cup (100 g) granulated sugar
  • 1/8 tsp (0.5 g) salt

Instructions

Par-bake the crust

  1. 1

    If your pie crust is frozen, thaw it per the package. Chill the crust (in its dish) in the refrigerator for about 30 minutes so it's cold going into the oven — a cold crust holds its shape and won't slump down the sides. Meanwhile, set an oven rack to the lowest position and preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).

  2. 2

    Line the chilled crust with a piece of parchment paper (crumple it first so it settles into the shape easily), then fill with pie weights or dried beans, pushing them gently up against the sides. Bake for 15 minutes, until the edges look set and are just starting to color.

  3. 3

    Carefully lift out the parchment and weights. Prick the bottom of the crust all over with a fork (this "docking" keeps it from puffing up). Return it to the oven and bake 7–8 minutes more, just until the bottom is beginning to brown. This is a partial bake — the crust finishes cooking later under the filling, so don't take it to full golden. Set aside and reduce the oven to 350°F (177°C).

Make the lemon filling

  1. 4

    Whisk the egg yolks together in a small bowl and set aside. In a medium saucepan over medium heat, whisk together the water, granulated sugar, cornstarch, salt, lemon juice, and lemon zest. It will look thin and cloudy at first, then thicken and start bubbling after about 6 minutes. Give it a whisk and reduce the heat to low.

  2. 5

    Temper the yolks: very slowly stream a few large spoonfuls of the hot lemon mixture into the beaten yolks, whisking constantly. Then, again slowly, whisk that yolk mixture back into the saucepan. Turn the heat back up to medium and cook until thick, with big bubbles bursting at the surface. Remove from heat and whisk in the butter. (Don't walk away during this step — it can thicken fast.)

  3. 6

    Spread the warm filling into the warm par-baked crust. Keep it warm while you whip the meringue — spreading meringue onto warm filling is what seals the two layers together and prevents weeping.

Meringue & bake

  1. 7

    With a handheld or stand mixer (whisk attachment), beat the egg whites and cream of tartar on medium speed for 1 minute, then raise to high and beat until soft peaks form, about 4 minutes more. Add the sugar and salt and keep beating on high until glossy, stiff peaks form, about 2 minutes more.

  2. 8

    Spread the meringue over the warm filling, making sure it touches the crust all the way around the edge — this seal is what keeps it from shrinking and weeping. Use the back of a spoon to pull up decorative peaks.

  3. 9

    Bake on the lowest rack at 350°F (177°C) for 20–25 minutes, until the meringue is toasty golden. If it browns too fast, loosely tent a piece of foil over the top without letting it touch the meringue. Cool on a wire rack at room temperature for 1 hour, then refrigerate for 4 hours before slicing and serving.

Notes: Even with a store-bought crust, the par-bake in steps 1–3 is not optional — skipping it gives a soggy bottom under the wet filling.  ·  Separate the 5 eggs while cold (they split cleaner), then let the whites sit out to reach room temperature — they whip faster and to greater volume.  ·  Best enjoyed on day 1; the meringue slowly weeps and softens with time.
