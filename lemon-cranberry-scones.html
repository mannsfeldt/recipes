Lemon Cranberry Scones with Lemon Glaze

Anna Olson's classic high-tea scone, studded with cranberries and finished with a tart lemon glaze.

  • Prep20 min
  • Cook25 min
  • Total45 min*
  • Yield8 scones
  • Servings8

Equipment

  • Baking tray
  • Parchment paper
  • Sieve
  • Pastry cutter (or two knives)
  • Whisk
Scale recipe

Ingredients

Scones

  • 2 cups (240 g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (50 g) sugar
  • 1 tbsp (14 g) baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp (1 g) fine salt
  • 1 tbsp (6 g) finely grated lemon zest
  • 1/2 cup (113 g) cool unsalted butter, cut into small pieces
  • 1/2 cup (120 g) whipping cream
  • 1/4 cup (60 g) 2% milk, plus extra for brushing
  • 1 large (50 g) egg
  • 1 tsp (4 g) vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (100 g) fresh or frozen cranberries

Glaze

  • 1 cup (120 g) icing sugar, sifted
  • 1 1/2 tbsp (22 g) lemon juice

Instructions

Scones

  1. 1

    Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C) and line a baking tray with parchment paper.

  2. 2

    Sift the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt into a large bowl. Stir in the lemon zest. Cut in the butter until the mixture is rough and crumbly, with small pieces of butter still visible.

  3. 3

    In a separate bowl, whisk the cream, milk, egg, and vanilla together. Add to the flour mixture and stir just until the dough comes together, then turn out onto a work surface and finish bringing it together by hand. Flatten the dough, press in the cranberries, and fold and flatten a few times to incorporate them.

  4. 4

    Shape the dough into a disc about 10 inches across. Cut into 8 wedges and place on the prepared tray, leaving a few inches between them. Brush with a little milk and bake 20–25 minutes, until lightly browned on top. Cool completely before glazing.

Glaze

  1. 5

    Whisk the icing sugar and lemon juice together and drizzle over the cooled scones with the whisk or a fork. Let the glaze set for an hour before serving.

Notes: Best enjoyed the day they're baked. *Total time above covers active prep and baking only — plan for an additional hour of glaze-setting time before serving.
