Apple Crisp

A simple, unfussy baked dessert — spiced apple filling under a buttery brown sugar and oat crumble. Best served warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

  • Prep15 min
  • Bake45 min
  • Total1 hr
  • Servings6–8

Equipment

  • 9×13-inch or 9×9-inch baking dish
  • Mixing bowl
Scale recipe

Ingredients

Apple Filling

  • 6 medium (900 g)apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
  • 1/2 cup (100 g)white sugar
  • 1 tsp (2.5 g)ground cinnamon

Crumble Topping

  • 1/2 cup (110 g)brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup (65 g)all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup (45 g)rolled oats
  • 1 tsp (2.5 g)ground cinnamon
  • 1/3 cup (75 g)cold butter, cut into small pieces

Instructions

  1. 1

    Preheat oven to 350 °F.

  2. 2

    Toss sliced apples with white sugar and cinnamon. Spread in an even layer in a 9×13-inch baking dish.

  3. 3

    In a bowl, combine brown sugar, flour, rolled oats, and cinnamon. Add the cold butter pieces and rub in with your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized chunks.

  4. 4

    Sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the apples.

  5. 5

    Bake 40–45 minutes until the topping is golden brown and the apples are bubbling and tender when pierced with a knife. Serve warm.

Notes: Use a mix of apples for the best flavour — a tart variety like Granny Smith paired with a sweeter one like Honeycrisp works beautifully. The crisp reheats well in the oven at 325 °F for 10–15 minutes.
