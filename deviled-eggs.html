Deviled Eggs

The classic potluck deviled egg — a smooth, tangy yolk filling of mayo, relish, and mustard piped back into the whites and dusted with paprika.

  • Prep15 min
  • Cook10 min
  • Total25 min
  • Yield24 halves
  • Servings12

Equipment

  • Saucepan
  • Mixing bowl
Scale recipe

Ingredients

  • 12 large (600 g) eggs
  • 1/3 cup (75 g) mayonnaise (or half mayo, half Greek yogurt)
  • 2 tbsp (30 g) pickle relish (or diced dill pickle)
  • 1 1/2 tsp (8 g) Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 tsp (1 g) salt, to taste
  • 1/8 tsp (1 g) black pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 tsp (1 g) paprika, for garnish

Instructions

  1. 1

    Place the eggs in a saucepan and cover with cold water. Bring to a boil, then stir in 1 tsp baking soda (it helps them peel), cover, and remove from the heat. Let sit 12 minutes, then transfer to an ice-water bath to cool.

  2. 2

    Peel the eggs and slice each in half lengthwise. Pop the yolks into a bowl.

  3. 3

    Add the mayonnaise, relish, and mustard to the yolks and mash smooth. Season with salt and pepper, tasting and adjusting.

  4. 4

    Spoon or pipe the filling back into the egg whites and sprinkle with paprika just before serving.

Notes: A teaspoon of baking soda in the boiling water makes the eggs far easier to peel. Half mayo and half plain Greek yogurt lightens the filling. Variations: fold in mashed avocado, crumbled bacon, or chives, or add cayenne or hot sauce for heat. Keeps refrigerated 2–3 days.
