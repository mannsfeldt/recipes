German Plum Cake (Pflaumenkuchen)

A traditional yeast-risen sheet cake topped with halved fresh plums and dusted with sugar straight from the oven — a classic summer and early-autumn bake.

  • Prep30 min
  • Rise1 hr 30 min
  • Bake25 min
  • Total2 hr 30 min
  • Servings12–16

Equipment

  • Stand mixer with dough hook (or hands)
  • Rimmed baking sheet (cookie sheet)
  • Small saucepan
Scale recipe

Ingredients

Dough

  • 3 1/2 cups (440 g)all-purpose flour
  • 1 envelope (7 g)instant or active dry yeast (2¼ tsp)
  • 1/3 cup (65 g)sugar
  • 1 pinch (2 g)salt
  • 3/4 cup (170 g)butter
  • 1 cup (240 ml)milk
  • 1 (50 g)egg
  • 1 tsp (4 g)vanilla extract

Topping

  • 2 lbs (900 g)fresh plums, pitted and halved
  • 3 tbsp (36 g)sugar, for sprinkling

Instructions

  1. 1

    Combine flour, yeast, sugar, and salt in a large bowl.

  2. 2

    Melt butter gently in a small saucepan over low heat. Remove from heat and let cool to lukewarm, then stir in milk, egg, and vanilla.

  3. 3

    Pour the wet ingredients into the dry and stir to combine. Knead by hand (or with a dough hook) for about 8 minutes until smooth and elastic.

  4. 4

    Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise in a warm spot until doubled, about 1 hour.

  5. 5

    Grease and flour a rimmed baking sheet. Press or roll the dough out to fit the sheet in an even layer.

  6. 6

    Arrange plum halves cut-side up, slightly overlapping, over the entire surface of the dough. Let rise again in a warm spot for 30 minutes.

  7. 7

    Preheat oven to 400 °F (or 350 °F convection). Bake 20–30 minutes until the crust is golden and the plums are tender and slightly caramelised.

  8. 8

    Immediately upon removing from the oven, sprinkle generously with sugar over the hot plums. The sugar dissolves into a light glaze.

Notes: Do not add sugar before baking — it goes on right after the cake comes out of the oven so it melts into the hot fruit. Ensure all ingredients are at room temperature for the best rise.
