Easy Instant Pot BBQ Ribs

Fall-off-the-bone baby back ribs cooked under pressure with a smoky-sweet dry rub, then broiled with barbecue sauce until sticky and caramelized.

  • Prep20 min
  • Cook25 min
  • Total45 min
  • Yield1 rack
  • Servings8

Equipment

  • 6-qt Instant Pot
  • Metal trivet
  • Rimmed baking sheet
Scale recipe

Ingredients

Dry rub

  • 1 tbsp (12 g) brown sugar
  • 2 tsp (10 g) kosher salt
  • 2 tsp (5 g) onion powder
  • 2 tsp (5 g) chili powder
  • 2 tsp (4 g) ground mustard
  • 1 1/2 tsp (3 g) smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp (1 g) dried thyme
  • 1 tsp (1 g) dried oregano
  • 1 tsp (3 g) garlic powder
  • 1 tsp (2 g) freshly ground black pepper
  • 3/4 tsp (2 g) ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp (1 g) cayenne pepper

Ribs + cooking

  • 2 pounds (907 g) baby back pork ribs, trimmed
  • 2 cups (480 g) chicken stock
  • 1/2 cup (120 g) apple cider vinegar
  • 2 cups (570 g) barbecue sauce

Instructions

Dry rub

  1. 1

    In a small bowl, combine the brown sugar, salt, onion powder, chili powder, ground mustard, smoked paprika, thyme, oregano, garlic powder, black pepper, cumin, and cayenne.

Ribs + cook

  1. 2

    Remove the membrane from the underside of the ribs, pulling it off along the whole length. Season the ribs all over with the rub, pressing it firmly into both sides. (You can cover and refrigerate overnight at this point.)

  2. 3

    Add the chicken stock and apple cider vinegar to a 6-qt Instant Pot and set the metal trivet inside. Stand the ribs upright on the trivet, wrapping them in a circle.

  3. 4

    Secure the lid, set to high pressure, and cook for 25 minutes. When it finishes, let the pressure release naturally for 10–15 minutes before quick-releasing the rest.

  4. 5

    Meanwhile, preheat the oven to broil and line a baking sheet with foil.

  5. 6

    Lay the ribs meat-side up in a single layer on the sheet and brush generously with barbecue sauce.

  6. 7

    Broil until the sauce is caramelized and browned, about 4–6 minutes. Serve with the remaining barbecue sauce.

Notes: Removing the membrane lets the rub and sauce reach the meat and keeps the ribs tender. Broiling is optional but worth it for the sticky, caramelized crust — watch closely, as it browns fast. For spare or St. Louis-style ribs, increase the pressure-cook time to 30–35 minutes.
