Slow Cooker Butternut Squash Soup

Rich, creamy, and naturally vegan — this hands-off slow cooker soup is silky smooth with a hint of apple sweetness and warm spice.

  • Prep15 min
  • Cook4 hr
  • Total4 hr 15 min
  • Servings6–8

Equipment

  • Large slow cooker
  • Immersion blender
Scale recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (475 ml)vegetable stock
  • 2 cloves (10 g)garlic, peeled and minced
  • 1 medium (100 g)carrot, peeled and diced
  • 1 (180 g)Granny Smith apple, cored and diced
  • 1 medium (800 g)butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and diced
  • 1 sprig (2 g)fresh sage
  • 1 medium (150 g)white onion, diced
  • 1/2 tsp (3 g)salt, or more to taste
  • 1/4 tsp (1 g)freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/8 tsp (0.3 g)cayenne, or more to taste
  • 1 pinch (0.5 g)ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch (0.5 g)ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup (120 ml)canned unsweetened coconut milk

Instructions

  1. 1

    Add vegetable stock, garlic, carrot, apple, butternut squash, sage, onion, salt, pepper, cayenne, cinnamon, and nutmeg to the slow cooker. Toss to combine.

  2. 2

    Cook on low 6–8 hours (or high 3–4 hours), until the squash is completely tender and mashes easily with a fork.

  3. 3

    Remove and discard the sage sprig. Stir in the coconut milk.

  4. 4

    Use an immersion blender to purée the soup until completely smooth. (Or transfer in batches to a regular blender, being careful with the hot liquid.) Taste and adjust seasoning.

  5. 5

    Serve warm, garnished with an extra drizzle of coconut milk and a pinch of cayenne or smoked paprika if desired.

Notes: Freezes beautifully — store in airtight containers for up to 3 months. Thin with extra stock if needed after reheating.
